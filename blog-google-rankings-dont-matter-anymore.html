What Are Google AI Overviews? (And Why They Matter More Than Rankings)

Google AI Overviews appear at the very top of search results—above all traditional rankings—and use generative AI to answer user questions in a few sentences. Think of them as featured snippets on steroids.

When someone searches "best plumber near me," Google AI Overviews might generate an answer like:

"Based on customer reviews and licensing verification, ABC Plumbing offers 24/7 emergency service with licensed technicians. They specialize in leak detection and water heater replacement, with same-day service available in most areas."

Notice what happened? The user got their answer. They might not click anything else.

The critical shift: Google AI Overviews cite sources, but most users never visit those cited websites. If your business isn't cited, you don't exist in that search.

Google rolled out AI Overviews widely in mid-2024, and early data suggests significant traffic impacts for many businesses. For many local businesses, this represented the single largest traffic drop in their history—even while maintaining top rankings.

How ChatGPT, Claude, and Perplexity Are Changing Search Behavior

But Google isn't the only problem. A massive portion of search traffic has moved off Google entirely.

Millions of people now use ChatGPT instead of Google for many queries. They're asking conversational questions like:

  • "I need a real estate agent who specializes in flood recovery properties in Houston—who's best?"
  • "What HVAC company in Phoenix offers the best warranty on new AC systems?"
  • "Which local contractor in Denver knows the most about energy-efficient home renovations?"

These platforms provide detailed answers, often with specific business recommendations. If your business isn't mentioned, you lose that customer to whoever is mentioned.

The difference from Google: ChatGPT and similar platforms don't show ten blue links. They provide one answer, citing 2-3 sources maximum. You're either in that answer or you're out.

This isn't about ranking #1 versus #2. It's about being cited by AI or being invisible.

Why Traditional SEO Doesn't Work for AI Platforms

Here's where most businesses get stuck: they keep optimizing for 2015 Google.

Traditional SEO focused on:

  • Keywords in meta tags (AI doesn't read meta descriptions)
  • Backlink quantity (AI values content quality over link count)
  • Keyword density (AI detects natural language, not keyword stuffing)
  • Page load speed (important, but not the deciding factor for citations)

AI platforms look for different signals entirely. They want:

  1. Direct answers to specific questions (not keyword-optimized fluff)
  2. Structured data (FAQ schema, HowTo schema, Article schema)
  3. Expertise and credentials (real human authority, not just content volume)
  4. Specific, factual claims with supporting evidence (AI can verify information)
  5. Local relevance signals (service area, years in business, local case examples)

Think of it this way: traditional SEO was about getting Google to find your page. Answer Engine Optimization (AEO) is about making AI trust your content enough to cite it as the authoritative answer.

Those are fundamentally different goals.

What It Actually Takes to Win AI Citations (And Why Most Businesses Fail)

Understanding what needs to happen and successfully executing it are two very different things. Here's what's actually involved in Answer Engine Optimization—and why most businesses who try DIY end up hiring specialists after wasting 6 months:

The Technical Infrastructure Challenge

AI platforms require multiple layers of structured data markup working together without conflicts. A single syntax error in your schema markup breaks the entire implementation—and most business owners don't discover the error for months because Google doesn't send you an error message.

Popular WordPress plugins often generate conflicting schema that AI platforms can't parse correctly. You might have Article schema from your theme, FAQ schema from a different plugin, and LocalBusiness schema from yet another tool—all creating duplicate or contradictory data that confuses AI systems.

The difference between "having schema markup" and "having schema markup that actually works for AI citations" is the difference between wasting time and getting results.

The Content Architecture Problem

Every piece of content needs explicit question-answer mapping with specific word counts, heading hierarchies, and semantic relationships. But here's the challenge: the same article must simultaneously satisfy Google's E-E-A-T requirements (which favor comprehensive, detailed content) AND conversational AI parsing (which favors concise, direct answers).

These are often contradictory optimization targets. What wins a featured snippet in Google might be too brief for ChatGPT to consider authoritative. What satisfies Claude's expertise requirements might be too detailed for Perplexity to extract a clean answer.

You're not optimizing for one system. You're optimizing for 5+ different platforms with different quality thresholds and different citation preferences.

The Authority Documentation Gap

AI platforms verify credentials before citing sources. This isn't about claiming expertise—it's about structuring your credentials in machine-readable formats that AI can validate.

That means specific schema types for author credentials, properly formatted case studies with measurable outcomes, service area documentation with geographic specificity, and expertise signals that pass AI verification checks.

Most businesses have the credentials. They just don't have them documented in ways that AI platforms can parse and verify. The technical difference matters enormously.

The Multi-Platform Testing Reality

What wins featured snippets in Google doesn't automatically get cited by ChatGPT. What works in ChatGPT might fail in Claude or Perplexity. Each platform weighs authority signals differently.

Real example: A real estate article might dominate Google AI Overviews but never get cited by ChatGPT because the credential formatting doesn't match ChatGPT's verification patterns. Or vice versa.

Testing and optimizing across multiple platforms requires systematic tracking, version testing, and knowing which signals each platform prioritizes. This isn't guesswork—it's methodical experimentation.

The Common DIY Mistakes That Waste Months

Most businesses attempting Answer Engine Optimization on their own hit one of these roadblocks:

Schema implementation errors: Incorrect syntax that breaks featured snippet eligibility entirely. Google won't tell you it's broken—you just won't see results.

Generic content optimization: Focusing on keywords instead of direct answer quality. AI platforms skip content that doesn't explicitly answer specific questions, no matter how well it ranks.

Missing credential verification: Having expertise but not documenting it in machine-readable formats. AI questions your authority because it can't verify your credentials automatically.

Single-platform optimization: Optimizing for Google while accidentally breaking ChatGPT compatibility, or vice versa. You need simultaneous multi-platform success.

Timeline underestimation: Assuming this takes a few weeks. In reality, learning the systems, implementing correctly, testing across platforms, and iterating based on results typically takes 6-12 months for someone doing it for the first time.

Why don't my Google rankings matter as much anymore?

What's the difference between SEO and Answer Engine Optimization?

Traditional SEO optimized to rank in search results (top 10 positions). Answer Engine Optimization (AEO) optimizes to be cited by AI as the authoritative answer—whether that's Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT, Claude, or Perplexity. SEO focused on keywords and backlinks. AEO focuses on structured data, direct answers, and demonstrable expertise that AI can verify and trust.

How do I know if AI platforms are citing my competitors instead of me?

Test it yourself. Ask ChatGPT, Claude, or Perplexity questions your customers would ask about your services in your area.
